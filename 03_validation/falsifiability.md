# Falsifiability — What Would Count Against CAP

CAP makes specific, checkable claims. This document lists what evidence would weaken or refute each claim. A framework that cannot specify what would falsify it is not yet a usable framework.

## Claim 1: Operators are consistently assignable

**Claim:** Independent analysts can converge on the same operator selection for the same situation, given the COM Grammar.

**What would falsify it:**
- Inter-rater agreement below chance level on operator selection across multiple analysts
- LLM models drift unpredictably on operator selection across runs of the same case
- Cases that admit multiple equally-defensible operators with no principled tiebreaker

**Current evidence:** Three independent LLM inference surfaces (`comet_12b_v.7-i1`, `silicon-maid-7b-imatrix`, `fimbulvetr-11b-v2`) match exactly on 8/8 COM Grammar cases. This is consistency evidence, not human inter-rater data.

**Open question:** Does this generalize to human analysts? Untested.

---

## Claim 2: Transition cost predicts perceived burden

**Claim:** Higher computed transition cost correlates with higher subjective burden of attempting the operator.

**What would falsify it:**
- High-cost operators feel routine to operators who attempt them
- Low-cost operators feel impossibly hard
- Self-reported burden does not correlate with cost classification

**Current evidence:** None — this requires human study with a cost-burden survey instrument.

**Open question:** Genuinely open. CAP has not yet been tested against subjective burden data.

---

## Claim 3: Budget-gated recommendations reduce failure rate

**Claim:** Recommendations that respect ObserverUsableBudget have lower rollback rate than recommendations given without budget awareness.

**What would falsify it:**
- Budget-gated recommendations show same or worse rollback rate
- Operators that violate the budget are completed successfully more often than predicted
- Budget signal is too noisy to gate on

**Current evidence:** None — requires longitudinal usage data.

**Open question:** Open. This is the strongest empirical claim and the hardest to test.

---

## Claim 4: Telemetry signals correlate with operator failure rate

**Claim:** Operators attempted under Overheating or Breach telemetry fail at higher rate than under Clean telemetry.

**What would falsify it:**
- Operator success rate is independent of telemetry state
- Telemetry-state classification is itself unreliable
- Operators succeed under Breach state at rates indistinguishable from Clean

**Current evidence:** None — requires controlled study.

---

## Claim 5: Adjustment Layer outperforms symptom-only repair

**Claim:** Routes generated by Adjustment Layer (which integrates upstream Looking-Glass diagnosis) have higher long-term stability than routes that address only the visible symptom.

**What would falsify it:**
- Symptom-only repairs have equivalent or better long-term stability
- Upstream-bridge identification is unreliable across analysts/models
- "Stability" cannot be operationalized for the relevant domains

**Current evidence:** None — this is a major open empirical claim.

---

## Claim 6: Anti-self-justification reduces dialogue drift

**Claim:** LLM dialogue systems that re-evaluate previous outputs by telemetry (rather than defending them) have measurably better outcomes (lower hallucination rate, less user friction, better user trust).

**What would falsify it:**
- Self-auditing systems perform same as defending systems on hallucination benchmarks
- Users prefer confident-sounding (even if wrong) responses
- Re-evaluation introduces latency that degrades user experience more than it helps

**Current evidence:** None — this is the LLM/RAG application claim and is not yet benchmarked.

---

## Claim 7: COM-Log format produces more useful output than free-form advice

**Claim:** Structured COM-Log output produces more actionable guidance than free-form prose.

**What would falsify it:**
- Users prefer prose recommendations
- COM-Log structure is too rigid for nuanced cases
- Concrete next-physical-step requirement filters out important interventions

**Current evidence:** Worked examples and three-model agreement on case classification. No user study.

---

## Summary

Of CAP's seven main claims, two have machine-checked consistency evidence (Claims 1 and 7). Five have no current empirical evidence and are open for falsification. CAP is research-only because of this.

**A research framework is honest about what it has not yet shown. CAP has shown that its boundary rules are computable and stable across inference surfaces. It has not yet shown that the framework itself produces better outcomes for any of its target domains.**

That work is the next phase.
